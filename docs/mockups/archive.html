"When does an AI become an agent?"

›

When it can take action on your behalf rather than just describing action with language. If it can only tell you what to do, it's a chatbot. If it can go do it, it's an agent.

"When does fine-tuning make a model worse?"

›

When you fine-tune on data that's narrower than the task you actually need it to perform. The model gets better at one thing by forgetting everything adjacent to it.

apr 5

"When is RAG the wrong answer?"

›

When the problem is about reasoning, not retrieval. RAG helps when you need the model to know something it wasn't trained on. It doesn't help when the model already has the knowledge but fails to use it correctly.

apr 4

"When does a hallucination become a bug?"

›

When it reaches a user. A hallucination in an eval is a data point. A hallucination in production that a user acts on is a product defect — the distinction is about consequence, not content.

apr 3

"When should you not use an LLM?"

›

When you need deterministic output. LLMs are probabilistic by nature — same input, different output. If your system requires the same answer every time, you want a lookup, a rules engine, or a traditional classifier.

apr 2

"When does 'AI-powered' mean nothing?"

›

When the AI is doing something a regex or a sort could do just as well. "AI-powered" means something when the task requires understanding context, nuance, or ambiguity — not when it's pattern matching dressed up in a press release.

apr 1

"When is a model done training?"

›

Technically, when loss stops improving. In practice, when the team runs out of compute budget, time, or patience. The idea of a "finished" model is mostly a business decision dressed up as a technical one.
