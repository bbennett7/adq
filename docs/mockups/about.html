About me

I'm a senior software engineer based in Los Angeles. I build AI systems for a living — content moderation pipelines, RAG infrastructure, the stuff that has to work at 3am when no one is watching.

I've been doing this long enough to have strong opinions about what works and what's theater. The more I build, the longer my list of questions gets — which is either a sign that AI is genuinely complicated, or that I'm getting better at noticing what I don't understand. Probably both.

I'm also building a side project, doing a lot of LeetCode, and growing a Meyer lemon tree that is doing better than expected. I live in LA with my partner, who is studying for her real estate license and is deeply unimpressed by transformers.

↳ terminally curious, professionally skeptical.
the site

About this place

The AI space moves fast, talks loud, and rewards confident-sounding nonsense. This site is for something different — the foundational, honest questions that seem basic until you actually try to answer them clearly.

One question a day. One honest answer. No hype, no frameworks, no thought leadership. Just the questions I'm genuinely asking while building these systems, and the clearest answers I can find.

The format: a "when does X" threshold question — not "what is X" but the harder version. When does a thing become the other thing? Those questions turn out to be much harder to answer with marketing language.

↳ one question at a time. it helps.
